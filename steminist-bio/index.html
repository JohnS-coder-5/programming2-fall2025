Katherine Johnson

Creola Katherine Johnson was a highly important figure in the early days of NASA and it's predecesor, NACA. She was described as a human computer, whose calculations of orbital mechanics were critical to US crewed spaceflights. She had a film called Hidden Figures made about her and 2 others(Dorothy Vaughan and Mary Jackson) about their underappreciated work at NASA.

A Short Summarization of Her Life

  1. Born in 1918 in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia
  2. Graduated from West Virginia State College with a degree in mathematics and French.
  3. Started at NASA (then NACA) in 1953 as a "human computer".
  4. Recognition and Legacy: She is remembered as a prominent inspirational figure and was inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame for her contributions to human spaceflight and equality

Some of her achhievements/awards include

  • Awarded the Congressional Gold Medal by the United States Congress
  • The Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • In 2021 she was Inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame
